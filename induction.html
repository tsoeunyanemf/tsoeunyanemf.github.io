Induction Module

The Induction Module is aimed to: a) welcome students to the course; b) allow students to get familiar with the Virtual Learning Environment (VLE) and get to know the other students; c) allow students to demonstrate competencies in the use of the tools available to online learners; d) allow students to demonstrate academic skills, e.g. essay/report writing, research techniques, referencing, reflective learning and critical thinking at a level commensurate with Postgraduate study.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the Module, I was able to:

  • Get familiar with the Virtual Learning Environment.
  • Get familiar with the Harvard Referencing Guide.
  • Have an understanding about the grading criteria of the course.
  • Understand two essential rules for the next assignments: a) It is recommended to include an equal number of industry reports and academic references; b) It is recommended to write the essay answering to all requirements in equal parts.
  • Create an account on Github and begin my studies about this amazing platform.

Assignment

Essay on the following topic - The importance of a Postgraduate Degree in the Computer Science field.

Your paper should:

  • Clearly introduce your field of study and the postgraduate degree (PGCert, PGDip or MSc) you are undertaking.
  • Explain the importance of this field and the postgraduate degree to the field. Include a reflection of your experience and/or observations.
  • Consider how taking on this postgraduate degree might affect the field and society at large. Include your expectations from completing the course.
  • Use Harvard Referencing Guide.

Resources

In the Induction Module I was introduced to amazing tools:

  • Codio
    Codio is a web-based Integrated Development Environment (IDE) for all the programming languages used during your course.

  • Github
    Used in all courses for team projects, collaborative coding and your e-portfolio. Github is a code hosting platform for version control and collaboration. GitHub is the platform of choice for developers from various large corporations. It is a cloud-based hosting service that lets you manage Git repositories.

  • Kopernio
    Kopernio is a free browser extension that enables one-click access to journal articles from thousands of academic websites. Link to the website: https://kopernio.com/

  • Turnitin
    Turnitin is an originality checking and plagiarism prevention service that checks your writing for citation mistakes or inappropriate copying.

  • Moodle
    The Virtual Learning Environment (VLE) is a central platform that provides access to a wide range of course materials, lets students communicate with each other and their tutors, submit assignments and a lot more.

I was so excited about the impacts on the society that I ended up writing the article about with 2.371 words about how Computational Thinking Education can reshape the world after COVID-19.

Workforce of the future: how Computational Thinking can reshape the world after COVID-19?

Summary
This paper aims to discuss "technological unemployment" in the current Age of Automation. The COVID-19 pandemic has accelerated the growth of Artificial Intelligence to automate work tasks. Although new technologies can replace jobs, it can also ignite mechanisms to open up new opportunities for workers. In the long run, automation can create more jobs than it replaces. In order for this to happen, unemployment and inequality caused by automation will require new frameworks for government cooperation and new educational systems to help workers develop new skills. The focus of this article is to examine some the most in-demand skills for the future of work.

I hope to publish this article in an educational magazine after having a feedback from my tutor. I am aware that this was not the aim of the module, but I simply love researching and writing.
